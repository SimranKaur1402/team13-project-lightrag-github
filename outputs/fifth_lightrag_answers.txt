- **Rationale**: Review the current oral medication regimen, including dosage, frequency, and adherence. Verify that the patient is taking the medications as prescribed and evaluate any potential barriers to effective therapy.

- **Action**: Adjust the dosage of the current oral medications if they are not at their maximum prescribed levels and if tolerated by the patient.

  - **Lifestyle Modifications**: Focus on weight management, smoking cessation, and regular physical activity to diminish risk factors.
  - **Medications**: Initiation of antihypertensive and lipid-lowering therapies is recommended to significantly lower cardiovascular risk.
  - **Monitoring**: Intensive management of blood pressure and lipid levels is essential since these factors contribute directly to macrovascular morbidity and mortality in patients with diabetes.

**Definition and Risk Factors**
- Microvascular complications involve small blood vessels and lead to conditions like diabetic retinopathy, nephropathy, and neuropathy. These complications are primarily related to chronic hyperglycaemia and include:
  - Duration of diabetes
